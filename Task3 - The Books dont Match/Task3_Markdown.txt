# Task 3 — The Books Don't Match

## Objective

Reconcile a known, hand-counted total against a messy digital payment history. The goal is to identify discrepancies, resolve ambiguous payment records using predefined rules, and determine which payments or people require follow-up.

---

## Scenario

You are the treasurer for a university class trip.

Each student is required to contribute **$25** toward the trip expenses.

After collecting payments, you manually counted the money received and verified the total.

### Known Correct Total

- **Expected Total Collected:** **$300**
- **Trip Fee per Student:** **$25**
- **Expected Number of Students:** **12**
- **Cash Collected:** **$75**
- **Expected Digital Payments:** **$225**

The reconciliation should confirm that the combined cash and valid digital payments equal **$300**.

---

## Files Provided

### `students.csv`

Contains the official list of students expected to pay.

Fields:

- Student ID
- Student Name
- Amount Due

---

### `payment_history.csv`

Contains the raw digital payment history exactly as exported from the payment platform.

Characteristics:

- Inconsistent sender names
- Nicknames
- Initials
- Blank memos
- Emoji memos
- Duplicate payment
- Unknown sender
- Non-trip transaction
- Cash deposit record

No preprocessing has been performed.

---

### `ground_truth.json`

Contains:

- Expected totals
- Payment amount
- Name mappings
- Validation rules
- Reconciliation constraints

---

## Interpretation Rules

Use the following rules when matching payments to students.

### Payment Rules

- A valid trip payment must be exactly **$25**.
- Ignore transactions with amounts other than **$25**.
- Ignore duplicate payments from the same student.
- Ignore payments from unknown senders unless they can be confidently identified.

## Expected Outcome

The reconciliation process should:

1. Match each payment to the correct student.
2. Detect duplicate payments.
3. Identify payments that cannot be matched.
4. Ignore unrelated transactions.
5. Verify whether the final reconciled total equals the known correct total.
6. Produce a report listing:
   - Successfully matched students
   - Duplicate payments
   - Unknown payments
   - Missing payments
   - Final reconciled total
   - Any discrepancies requiring follow-up

---

## Success Criteria

A successful reconciliation should:

- Confirm that valid payments plus cash equal **$300**.
- Explain every unmatched transaction.
- Identify any duplicate payments.
- List any students who appear unpaid.
- Clearly document how ambiguous names were resolved.
